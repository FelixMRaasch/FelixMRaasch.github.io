I've done a lot of talking to people about what career options after leaving university. One question that comes up a lot in conversation is "Can you code?" It's a question thats always stumped me. On the one hand I've been exposed to a variety of different programming languages in different environment setups. Using R and R Markdown in R Studio for statistics, numpy for linear algebra in jupyter notebooks and matplotlib for visualising solutions to differential equations. But on the other hand I've never had formal training in computer science concepts such as algorithms or data structures. Usually in these conversations I'd give a convoluted answer that in the mind of the asker amounted to a "no". 

An opportunity to build my own software in a live commercial setting was presented to me whilst working for a retail company in Wellington, NZ. After doing some data entry for the firm the boss mentioned that their app broke years ago and he needed it to be rebuilt. His problem was in dealing with software professionals, difficult to get hold of often over e-mail, expensive, opaque and often outsourcing their work and getting results that broke quickly. He said he was happy to let me build the app for him. the concept is very simple, a digit card that ... The code was going to be simple, expecially with generative AI. But what he needed was someone who can understand the process of getting from an idea to a finished product, and I was ready to make myself bridge that gap.
